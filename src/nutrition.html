Baseball Nutrition Plan

Eat like a player who wants energy, focus & recovery

This is not a diet program. It's simple food guidance for young baseball players and parents — built to support energy, growth, focus, recovery, training consistency, and game-day confidence.

Nutrition Rule

Young athletes need fuel, not extreme diets

A balanced baseball plate: carbohydrates for energy, protein for growth and repair, healthy fats for steady fuel, fruits and vegetables for vitamins and minerals, and water for hydration. Young athletes generally don't need special supplements — they need real food, enough calories, and consistent hydration.

Pre-game / pre-cage

3–4 hours before

Balanced meal: carb + lean protein + fruit/veg + water. E.g. chicken & rice with fruit, turkey sandwich with pretzels, pasta with lean meat sauce, eggs & potatoes & toast.

Quick snack

30–60 min before

Light and easy to digest: banana, applesauce, granola bar, pretzels, toast, crackers, fruit smoothie, low-fat yogurt. Avoid heavy, greasy meals right before training.

After training

Within 30–60 min

Carbs + protein + fluids: chocolate milk, yogurt & fruit, turkey sandwich, rice & eggs, chicken wrap, smoothie, banana with peanut butter.

Sample Training Day

A full day of fuel

Breakfast

Oatmeal with banana, eggs or yogurt, water.

Lunch

Turkey sandwich, fruit, pretzels or rice, water.

Pre-cage + recovery

Banana or granola bar before; chocolate milk or smoothie after.

Dinner

Lean protein, rice/pasta/potatoes, vegetables, water.

Parent reminder: this is general sports nutrition guidance, not medical advice. Talk to a pediatrician or registered dietitian for allergies, medical conditions, weight concerns, diabetes, eating issues, or specialized needs.
